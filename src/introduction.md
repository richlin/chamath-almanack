# Introduction

This book is a collection of Chamath Palihapitiya's ideas, in his own words. It is modeled after Eric Jorgenson's *The Almanack of Naval Ravikant* — organized by theme, built from direct quotes, with minimal editorial framing.

The source material comes from three solo videos where Chamath speaks directly to camera about the things he's learned across 30 years of building, investing, and thinking about the world. No co-hosts, no interruptions, no debate format — just Chamath distilling his experience into the clearest form he can.

The quotes are his exact words, lightly cleaned for readability (removing filler, fixing transcription artifacts), but never paraphrased. Where editorial context is provided, it's clearly separated from his speech.

The ideas span two domains: how to live and how to invest. They are connected by a single thread — Chamath's belief that life is an infinite game, and the only mistake is stopping.
